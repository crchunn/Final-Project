    More Fun Spotify Visuals.


    These Tableau visuals graph the average danceability, explicit, popularity, and energy for each year. Danceability is how suitable a track is for dancing, based on a combination of tempo, rhythm stability, beat strength and overall regularity. Energy is a measure of intensity and activity, a track that is fast, loud and noisy. These indicators, including speechiness(which includes, talk shows, audio books and rap), often combine for a perfect storm to drive popularity. The more popular a song, the more streams and in the end, more revenue for all.

    Based on the graphs, it is interesting to note that recent songs are more danceable, explicit, and popular while the energy has decreased from its peak in 2010. Another thing to note is the sum of the most popular songs per year. The years with the most popular songs are from the past 5 years as more people use spotify to listen to music. 2020 was not in the top 5 likely because of covid and a decrease in recorded songs. It is also interesting to compare these visuals to the matplotlib graphs. I wanted to see how these graphs were similar in different while showing similar data. Tableau and matplotlib are both helpful tools when using visuals for data. These visuals were based on a CSV from Kaggle.
