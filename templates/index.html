    Forecasting the Next Big Hit

    Using Machine Learning to determine whether or not the next hit is danceable.

    Predicting traits of popular songs on Spotify.


    Using information regarding Spotify's most popular songs so far in 2020, as well as the last 20 years, to indicate what components of songs will drive popularity of songs in 2021 in the United States.


    What makes a song popular? What is the driving force behind a beat that entices individuals to listen over and over again? Even more important to the music industry, what is the magic formula that propels consumers to purchase a track or album? What is it that will convert a consumer into a lifelong fan? According to Psychology Today, "a song needs to be similar enough 'to evoke the warm glow of familiarity' but atypical enough to 'feel new and exciting,'" which may be why remixes are so popular. They also report that lyrical differentation is important for success. However, for dance music, and pop, there is less of a relationship between lyrics and popularity. Columbia Business School puts forth that the sonic features of a song, "like beat, “acousticness,” and “valence,” or "mood" play an important role in the popularity of a song. They also say, "a hit song, or any other cultural product – like a film, or a novel — can’t simply be reverse engineered from what’s been popular in the past. Popular success really is more art than science."



    Citation: Psychology Today, 06/2018; Berger, J., & Packard, G. (in press). Are atypical things more popular? Psychological Science. doi:10.1177/0956797618759465. Columbia Business School articles "What Makes a Hit," by Colin Morris; “What Makes Popular Culture Popular? Product Features and Optimal Differentiation in Music,” by Michael Mauskapf and Noah Askin.

    While we may not be able to explain the psyche of the musical brain, we can look at the popular songs of the past and analyze to see, not only what makes them popular, but use machine learning to create a blueprint for future music.

    Using machine learning, we can make predictions using neural networks such as deep learning. We can also use regression to determine variance through our r-squared value. We can also use different models such as the Lasso Model, Ridge Model, and ElasticNet Model to calculate variance and compare our r-squared values from each model. Decision Trees and Random Forests can also be implemented to predict our data and through Random Forests, we can see which features contribute the most to our predicted score. KNN is another model that can help us predict our data. Based on a k-value, we can determine the class a data point will belong to and classify our data point. Machine learning is a fun way to predict our data.
