    What makes a song popular? What is the driving force behind a beat that entices individuals to listen over and over again? Even more important to the music industry, what is the magic formula that propels consumers to purchase a track or album? What is it that will convert a consumer into a life long fan? According to Pychology Today "a song needs to be similar enough 'to evoke the warm glow of familiarity' but atypical enough to 'feel new and exciting'", which may be why remixes are so popular. They also report that lyrical differentation is important for success. However, for dance music, and pop, there is less of a relationship between lyrics and popularity. Columbia Business School puts forth that the sonic features of a song, "like beat, “acousticness,” and “valence,” or mood" play an important role in the popularity of a song. They also say, "a hit song, or any other cultural product – like a film, or a novel — can’t simply be reverse engineered from what’s been popular in the past. Popular success really is more art than science."
    Citation: Psychology Today, 06/2018; Berger, J., & Packard, G. (in press). Are atypical things more popular? Psychological Science. doi:10.1177/0956797618759465. Columbia Business School articles "What Makes a Hit," by Colin Morris; “What Makes Popular Culture Popular? Product Features and Optimal Differentiation in Music,” by Michael Mauskapf and Noah Askin.
